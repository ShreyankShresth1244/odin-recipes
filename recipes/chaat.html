CHAAT


RECIPE

Ingredients: For the papri (crispy crackers): 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida) 1 tablespoon semolina (sooji) 1/4 teaspoon baking soda A pinch of salt 2 tablespoons vegetable oil Cold water (as needed) For assembling the chaat: 1 cup boiled chickpeas (or canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed) 1 large potato, boiled, peeled, and diced 1/2 cup plain yogurt (whisked until smooth) 1/4 cup tamarind chutney (sweet and tangy) 1/4 cup green chutney (coriander and mint chutney) 1 small onion, finely chopped 1 small tomato, finely chopped 1 green chili, finely chopped (optional) 1/4 cup chopped fresh coriander leaves (cilantro) Chaat masala (available in Indian stores or online) Roasted cumin powder Red chili powder (optional, for extra heat) Sev (thin crispy noodles made from chickpea flour) for garnish Pomegranate seeds for garnish (optional) Chopped fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions: Prepare the papri: In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, semolina, baking soda, salt, and vegetable oil. Mix well until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Gradually add cold water and knead the dough until it's firm and smooth. Cover the dough and let it rest for 10-15 minutes. Preheat the oven to 180°C (356°F). Roll out the rested dough into a thin sheet and cut it into small, even-sized circles or squares. Prick the papris with a fork to prevent them from puffing up while baking. Place the papris on a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes or until they turn golden brown and crispy. Assemble the papri chaat: In a large serving plate or individual bowls, arrange the papris. Top them with boiled chickpeas and diced boiled potatoes. Drizzle the whisked yogurt over the papris, chickpeas, and potatoes. Follow with tamarind chutney and green chutney as per your taste preference. Sprinkle chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies (if using), and fresh coriander leaves over the chaat. Generously sprinkle chaat masala, roasted cumin powder, and red chili powder (if desired) over the chaat for added flavor. Garnish the papri chaat with a handful of sev and pomegranate seeds (if using). Add some chopped fresh mint leaves for a refreshing touch. Serve the papri chaat immediately and enjoy the explosion of flavors and textures! Papri chaat is a delightful appetizer or snack that's perfect for gatherings and parties. It's sure to be a hit among your family and friends!
